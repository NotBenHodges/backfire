Americans move in and out of poverty

Millions of Americans move in and out of poverty throughout their lifetimes.
A study done by the Urban Institute in 2010 demonstrated that over half the
United States population will live in poverty at some point before 65 years of
age. More than 10.5 million people who live in poverty were a part of the
"working poor" in 2010. This means that they were in the labor force for at
least 27 weeks according to the U.S. Department of Labor, Bereau of Labor
Statistics. One common myth of poverty in the U.S. is that those who are in
poverty are fine with the living conditions they have. However, numerous
studies, including those listed above, point to the contrary. These stereotypes
arise from the idea that low income means low work ethic, but the truth is
that many are working to get above the poverty line and not everyone stays
at the same level forever.
